About Us

Our Origin

Anjuman e Saifee Hyderabad began in 1959 under the guidance of His Holiness Dr. Syedna Mohammad Burhanuddin (TUS) to serve the Dawoodi Bohra community. Initially operating informally, the trust was formally registered on August 9, 2000 (and re-registered under the Sindh Trust Act, 2020). From its earliest days, the organization focused on maintaining mosques and madrasas, organizing religious gatherings, and supporting families in need. Over time, the trust expanded its mission to include comprehensive welfare initiatives—education, healthcare, and community services—ensuring that every household has access to spiritual guidance and essential support.

Our Mission & Vision

Vision: To work for the welfare of both the Dawoodi Bohra community and the general public, fostering social responsibility, mutual cooperation, and spiritual growth under the guidance of the Dai al-Mutlaq.

Mission: To uplift needy members of the Jammat by maintaining mosques and community centers, organizing religious and educational programs, and providing essential support—ranging from daily Faiz ul Mawaid tiffins to comprehensive Deeni Taleem and charitable assistance—so that every individual can access spiritual guidance, quality education, and dignified welfare services.

What We Do

Religious Expenses

We cover all mosque upkeep costs—including utilities, maintenance, and staff salaries—along with Niaz meals during Muharram/Ramadan and daily Faiz ul Mawaid tiffins to every Dawoodi Bohra household.

Community Centers

Funding for Jamaat Khana utilities, repairs, and staffing—providing safe, welcoming spaces for religious gatherings, cultural events, and educational sessions.

Deeni Taleem

Comprehensive religious education for youth: Qur’an memorization (Hifz), Tajweed, Arabic, Fiqh, and character development—aiming to produce at least one Hafiz from every household.

Charity & Donations

Providing medical assistance, home renovation grants, scholarships, and housing support—ensuring financial aid for healthcare, education, and stable living conditions among needy families.
