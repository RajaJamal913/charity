What We Are Doing

Our Key Initiatives for Community Welfare

Religious Expenses

Covers all mosque upkeep costs—utilities, repairs, staff salaries—plus Niaz meals during Muharram/Ramadan and daily Faiz ul Mawaid tiffins to every Dawoodi Bohra household.

Community Centers

Funds to run and maintain Jamaat Khanas: utilities, repairs, and staff—providing a safe, welcoming space for religious gatherings, cultural events, and educational sessions.

Deeni Taleem

Comprehensive Islamic education for youth: Qur’an memorization (Hifz), Tajweed, Arabic language, Fiqh, Akhbar, Falsafat, and character development—aiming for at least one Hafiz per household.

Charity & Donations

Provides medical assistance, home renovation grants, scholarships, and housing support—ensuring financial aid for healthcare, education, and stable living conditions among needy families.

About Our Trust

Anjuman e Saifee Hyderabad: Empowering the Dawoodi Bohra Community

Anjuman e Saifee Hyderabad was founded in 1959 by His Holiness Dr. Syedna Mohammad Burhanuddin (TUS) and formally registered under the Trust Act on August 9, 2000 (re-registered under Sindh Trust Act 2020). Under the guidance of the Dai al-Mutlaq, our non-profit trust maintains mosques, madrasas, and community centers across Hyderabad, organizing religious gatherings and major events while promoting social welfare initiatives.

Guided by our Vision to serve the welfare of both our community and the general public—and our Mission to uplift needy members of the Jammat and promote comprehensive education—we focus on four core pillars: Religious Expenses (mosque upkeep, Niaz meals, Faiz ul Mawaid), Community Centers (Jamaat Khana utilities, maintenance, programming), Deeni Taleem (Qur’an memorization, Arabic, Fiqh, character development), and Charity & Donations (medical aid, home renovations, scholarships, housing support).

Since October 2021, Anjuman e Saifee Hyderabad has expanded its outreach: running daily Faiz ul Mawaid tiffins to every Dawoodi Bohra household, supporting over 85 Deeni Taleem students, and providing critical assistance to dozens of under-privileged families. Through these efforts, we strengthen loyalty to Dawat, foster mutual cooperation, and ensure every member has access to spiritual, educational, and financial resources.
